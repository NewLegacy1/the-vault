# Prop-Firm Landscape 2026: Apex, Topstep, Take Profit Trader, MyFundedFutures

## Key findings

- **The industry re-wrote its rules between July 2025 and March 2026 — most pre-2026 knowledge is stale.** Apex overhauled everything on 2026-03-01 ("4.0": one-time eval payments, EOD-or-intraday drawdown choice at purchase, consistency 30%→50%, MAE rule removed, payout cap of 6 per account). Topstep cut new traders to 90/10 in Jan 2026 and split funded payouts into Standard/Consistency paths. MFFU retired Starter/Expert/Milestone in July 2025 for Core/Rapid/Pro (+Flex/Builder). (tradecovex rules hub; propfirmsfinder; proptradingvibes — all secondary but corroborating; verify on firm help centers before purchase.)
- **EOD vs intraday trailing is the single highest-impact rule difference.** EOD trailing (floor moves only on closing balance): Topstep MLL, Apex EOD variant, MFFU Core/Pro, TPT Test & PRO+. Intraday trailing (floor ratchets on unrealized peak equity — a spike you don't bank still raises your floor): Apex Intraday variant, **TPT PRO (the funded stage!)**, MFFU Rapid. TPT's Test-is-EOD-but-PRO-is-intraday switch is the most cited funded-account killer (TPT help center — primary source; fundedwiki). Facts from rule pages.
- **All four firms now lock the trailing floor once it reaches starting balance (+$0–100).** Surviving to breakeven-lock converts the account from a $2–2.5k-bankroll problem into a free option. Apex Safety Net = drawdown + $100, lifetime; MFFU/Topstep floor stops at starting balance. Fact.
- **Consistency rules (funded): Apex 50% (no day ≥50% of profit since last payout), Topstep 40% on Consistency Path / none on Standard, MFFU Core 40–50% / Rapid & Pro none, TPT none once funded (50% during Test only).** A 1:5-geometry strategy with ~15 trades/month will regularly produce single days >50% of a cycle's profit — consistency rules are a *binding* constraint for this style, not fine print. Facts; the implication is analysis.
- **Payout mechanics diverge sharply.** Apex: weekly requests, $500 min, 5 qualifying days ($100–350/day minimum by size), rising per-payout ladder caps, **hard 6-payout lifetime cap then the PA closes** (100% split under 4.0 per tradecovex — conflicting reports of "100% first $25k then 90%" persist; verify). TPT: withdraw from day one, daily, 80/20, but only above a buffer = drawdown amount; PRO+ (after $10k cumulative) = 90/10, EOD, no buffer. Topstep: 90/10, bi-weekly windows or winning-day gates, no lifetime cap. MFFU: Core 80/20 weekly-ish ($5k/cycle cap), Rapid 90/10 with daily-to-5-winning-day cadence, Pro 80/20 bi-weekly, $1k min, $100k cumulative cap. Facts, secondary-sourced.
- **Cost structures:** Apex ~$197 one-time eval (chronic 70–90% promos) + **$85/mo per PA** — the only firm here with a materially recurring funded cost. TPT: monthly eval + one-time $130 activation, then $0. MFFU: monthly eval ($77/mo Core 50k), $0 activation, $0 funded. Topstep: monthly Combine, activation fee on XFA (a no-activation-fee Combine variant exists). Facts.
- **Stacking is legal everywhere but capped and copy-trade-restricted.** Apex: up to 20 accounts (deep-discount culture built around it). MFFU: 5 funded 50k or 3 funded 100k/150k; identical coordinated execution across accounts is grounds for termination. Topstep: 5 XFAs. TPT: 5 combined PRO/PRO+, copy trading only on PRO+ (≤5 accounts). Facts.

**Apex payout gauntlet in sequence** (propfirmsfinder, h2tfunding): balance > Safety Net + amount → 5 qualifying days (e.g. $250/day on 50k EOD) → no day ≥50% of cycle profit → ladder cap for that payout number → after payout #6 the PA closes and you re-evaluate. Bracket orders (attached stop) mandatory since March 2026.

**TPT buffer subtlety**: "withdraw day one" is gated — on a 50k with $2k DD you must reach $52k before the 80% split applies to withdrawals; profits extracted inside the buffer at account closure follow a 60-trading-day rule. PRO+ (unlockable at $10k cumulative PRO profit) removes the buffer and reverts to EOD trailing — **TPT PRO+ is arguably the best steady-state seat in the table** (90/10, EOD, no consistency, daily payouts), but you must survive intraday-trailing PRO to reach it.

**Stacking economics.** Stacking multiplies eval/monthly fees and discipline load, not edge. Rough EV frame: with per-account pass probability p and funded-stage expected extraction E, stacking n accounts scales gross linearly but (a) Apex's $85/mo/PA makes idle PAs bleed ($510/PA per 6 months), (b) consistency rules bind harder when attention splits, (c) identical-execution bans mean genuinely independent management (staggered entries at minimum — firms tolerate manual near-simultaneous entry differently; check each ToS). The commonly-cited sweet spot is 2–3 accounts at one EOD-drawdown firm rather than max-stacking Apex, unless running Apex's discount cycle deliberately. (Opinion, synthesized.)

**Trust flag**: all comparison sites used here (tradecovex, proptradingvibes, h2tfunding, propfirmsfinder, fundedwiki, traderpayout) are affiliate-monetized. Facts were cross-checked across ≥2 of them plus primary help-center pages where reachable (TPT Zendesk, MFFU Intercom). Numbers that conflict between sources are marked. Firm rules have changed 2–3× in 18 months; **re-pull the firm's own help center the week of any purchase.**

## APPLICATION TO THE VAULT

- **The current Apex-style intraday-trailing assumption may be pessimistic — and choosable.** Under Apex 4.0 the EOD variant exists at purchase time for the same sizes. For a 1:5-geometry strategy whose winners run 50–150 pts of open profit, intraday trailing taxes exactly the strategy's engine (unrealized excursion ratchets the floor before profit banks). **Every new evaluation should be bought as EOD-trailing.** If the live account is intraday-trailing, that materially changes the path-MC: the MC must trail the floor on per-trade MFE, not on daily closes.
- **The 50% consistency rule is a real constraint for a 15-trade/month, 1:5 strategy.** One +5R day ($600–700 at $150 risk) against a $1,200 cycle easily breaches 50%. Payout timing becomes a scheduling problem: either accumulate more qualifying days before requesting, or size cycles so no day dominates. Worth encoding in the income model: payouts lag equity by roughly one extra week per cycle.
- **Apex's 6-payout lifetime cap changes the income math**: each PA is a finite instrument (~6 × ladder caps), then a new eval. Amortize eval + $85/mo PA fees across ≤6 payouts when computing E[$/wk] — the path MC should model account *cycling*, not a perpetual account.
- **Ranked fit for this user's profile (EOD-preference, 1:5 geometry, small n, wants weekly-ish income):** 1) MFFU Pro or Core (EOD, $0 funded fees, light/no consistency) and TPT PRO+ as destination seat; 2) Apex EOD 4.0 (best headline split, but $85/mo + 6-payout cap + 50% consistency); 3) Topstep Standard path (no consistency, but 90/10 and daily loss limit); avoid: TPT PRO stage and MFFU Rapid unless the strategy is re-verified under intraday trailing. This ranking is opinion built on the cited facts.
- **Floor-lock is the strategic objective in every case**: all four firms free-roll the account once balance − floor ≥ drawdown (+$100). The sizing note's buffer-based Kelly logic implies trading *smaller* than max until lock, then re-rating. The first ~$2,100–2,600 of profit is worth more than any payout — it converts a 5–8%-of-buffer/trade proposition into a costless option.
