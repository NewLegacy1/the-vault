# Judas Swing & Manipulation Legs

## Key findings

- **Original ICT definition (Market Maker Primer Course, "Understanding The ICT Judas Swing"):** a form of engineered price manipulation where *both* buyers and sellers are deliberately driven to the wrong side of the market to create liquidity for smart money — a false directional move at/after the session open, against the true daily bias (ICT Sharks forum notes on the Primer lesson).
- **Canonical timing in the original lesson: New York midnight (00:00) to ~5:00 AM NY** — i.e., the London-open window, not the NY open. The bullish template: price runs *above* the Asian range high, then drops below the NY midnight open and the Asian low, then trends up for the day; bearish is the mirror (Market Maker Primer notes; tradingfinder.com).
- **ICT explicitly calls the concept fractal** — it applies to every session open and every timeframe (Market Maker Primer notes). This is the primary-source hook that legitimizes a "NY-open Judas."
- **The NY-session version (community synthesis of ICT's 2022 mentorship / Silver Bullet content):** the NY Judas fires roughly **8:30–10:00 AM ET**, sweeping the London high/low or the 8:00–8:30 pre-market consolidation extreme; the **9:30 equity open is the usual catalyst**; a market-structure shift typically confirms around the **9:50 macro**; the **10:00–11:00 Silver Bullet window** is where the post-Judas entry fills (ictkillzone.com; grandalgo.com; liquidityscan.io). *This exact 9:30→9:50→10:00 sequence is community codification — the pieces (Judas, macros, Silver Bullet) are ICT's, the packaged timeline is not a single ICT lecture.*
- **Structure of the pattern (ICT's AMD framing):** Accumulation (quiet range, e.g. Asia or pre-market) → **Manipulation (the Judas leg)** → Distribution (the true move). The Judas *is* the manipulation phase given a name.
- **Etymology:** ICT ties the name to betrayal — the move that leads traders in and abandons them. (Community sources variously cite Judas Iscariot or the "Judas goat"; the biblical framing is the one ICT himself uses.)
- **No statistics exist.** ICT publishes no frequency or success-rate data for Judas swings; claims like "reversals outside kill zones fail at substantially higher rates" are directionally plausible but unmeasured folklore.

## Details / mechanics

### The original (London) template
1. Establish daily bias from HTF (this is a precondition — without bias, a Judas is indistinguishable from a real breakout).
2. Mark the Asian range high/low and the NY midnight open price.
3. In the 00:00–05:00 NY window (most often after the London open), price runs against the bias — above the Asian high on a bearish day, below the Asian low on a bullish day — taking retail stops/breakout entries.
4. Confirmation the leg was a Judas: sweep of the liquidity pool, sharp displacement back, market structure shift (MSS/CHoCH) on the lower timeframe, price returning through the midnight open.
5. The true daily move (distribution) then runs the opposite side of the range.

### The NY AM version (relevant to the Vault)
- Reference levels shift: instead of the Asian range, the NY Judas targets **London session high/low** and the **pre-market (8:00–9:30) consolidation extremes**.
- Typical sequence on NQ/MNQ per community codification: 8:30 news or 9:30 cash open spikes through the pre-market high/low (the sweep) → body closes back inside the range → **~9:48–9:50** MSS on 1m/5m leaving an FVG → **10:00–10:10** retrace into that FVG fills the entry → distribution into 11:00–12:00.
- Entry conventions: limit at the FVG (often its CE); **stop just beyond the Judas extreme** (the sweep wick); first target the opposite-side liquidity pool (e.g., pre-market low / London low).
- ICT's rule of thumb from the framework: do not trade *during* the manipulation leg; trade only after the sweep + MSS confirm which move was false.

## APPLICATION TO THE VAULT

- **Your 10:00 rejection-block entry sits exactly where the framework says the Judas resolves.** In the NY-AM template, 9:30–10:00 is the manipulation window and 10:00 is when the false move has typically completed and reversed. A 1m rejection block printing at the 10:00 key open is, in ICT terms, the *distribution-side confirmation* of a 9:30 Judas — the wick you're entering on is often the tail end of the manipulation leg itself. This confirms rather than contradicts your timing; it also explains why entries *before* 10:00 in the same direction would be fighting the manipulation leg.
- **Stop-beyond-block-extreme = stop-beyond-Judas-extreme.** Your existing convention matches the community/ICT entry convention for post-Judas trades exactly. The one refinement the framework suggests: the invalidation is the *sweep* extreme, so if your rejection block's wick did not take out a discrete liquidity pool (pre-market high/low, London high/low, or overnight high/low), the setup is a lower grade — worth a tag in the PRB checklist ("swept level: Y/N + which").
- **Bias precondition:** ICT is emphatic that a Judas is only readable against a pre-established daily bias. Your Morningstar daily-bias checklist is the right gate; a 10:00 rejection block *against* the bias should be logged separately because in Judas terms it may itself be the trap.
- **For the NWOG sleeve:** a 9:30–10:00 sweep that terminates *into* an NWOG edge/CE and rejects is the manipulation leg and the HTF draw coinciding — this is the highest-confluence version of your "NWOG reversal at HTF level" observation and worth flagging as its own census category.
