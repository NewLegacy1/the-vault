# ICT Weekly Range Profiles & Premium/Discount (Equilibrium) Bias

## Key findings

- **Primary source secured**: "ICT Mentorship Core Content — Month 07 — Short Term Trading: Defining Weekly Range Profiles" (2017 mentorship lesson, uploaded free on The Inner Circle Trader YouTube channel 2022-09-27, 11 min). Full auto-caption transcript extracted via yt-dlp and archived at `vault-app/data/ict-transcripts/weekly-range-profiles-month07.txt`. All canon claims below are quoted from it.
- **Canon = 12 profiles (6 bull/bear pairs), and the classic is the *Tuesday* low, not a Wednesday Judas.** "The classic Tuesday low of the week… generally bullish… price hovering above a higher-timeframe discount array on Monday, then drops into [it Tuesday]… Tuesday London open and/or New York session." Pairs: Classic Tuesday Low/High · Wednesday Low/High · Consolidation Thursday Reversal (news/rate-driven, ~2 PM NY) · Consolidation Midweek Rally/Decline · Seek & Destroy Friday (bull/bear, "neutral or low probability… better to avoid trading these conditions altogether") · Wednesday Weekly Reversal (at long-term/intermediate-term extremes).
- **The engine is premium/discount arrays, not the weekday.** Every profile is defined as *when price finally reaches the higher-timeframe array*: "you anticipate this by knowing the higher-timeframe discount arrays, and when the market fails to drop into that array [Monday], odds are Tuesday will likely see the drive lower." The weekday labels are just the most common arrival days. Profiles answer **when** the weekly extreme forms; premium/discount answers **where**.
- **Premium/discount canon**: mark a dealing range (significant swing high↔low); the 50% midpoint is **equilibrium**; above = premium (algorithm sells / draws upward end there in bearish delivery), below = discount (buys). Buy below EQ in bullish contexts, sell above EQ in bearish ones — as a *filter*, not a signal. The 0.62/0.705/0.79 OTE levels live in the deep half (cross-ref `ote-optimal-trade-entry.md`). Powell's "KO + OB at equilibrium" confluence in `high-probability-conditions` is the same idea one level down.
- **ICT explicitly frames profiles as hindsight-study templates, with zero statistics**: "if you just look through it quickly… it's going to seem unfruitful, but its impact is shown and solidified by you going through price data and seeing these profiles unfold in hindsight." No frequency data ("classic Tuesday low occurs X% of bullish weeks") exists anywhere in the primary record.
- **The popular "Mon–Tue accumulation → Wednesday Judas → Thu–Fri distribution" 5-day AMD template is community packaging, not the Month 07 canon.** Canon's *most classic* profile puts the weekly low on **Tuesday**; the community template moved the manipulation to Wednesday and hardened "Monday = don't trade" into a rule ICT never states in this lesson. Both exist in circulation; know which one you're quoting.
- **On Mondays specifically, canon is agnostic-to-mildly-useful, not prohibitive.** Monday's canonical role is "hovering" near the HTF array (positioning) — the weekly *extreme* rarely prints Monday in the taught profiles, but nothing in the primary lesson forbids intraday trading on Monday. Several profiles even have Monday trending ("Monday and Tuesday can also be down days as well to form this [Wednesday low] profile"). The hard "skip Monday" rule is community lore — and note Powell's Monday-skip is explicitly his own journal stats, not doctrine (see `powell-trading-psychology.md`).

## Details / mechanics

### The 12 profiles (canon, Month 07)
| Profile (bull / bear) | Mon–Wed behavior | Weekly extreme forms | Precondition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Classic Tuesday Low / High | Mon hovers above discount (below premium) array | **Tuesday**, London and/or NY; NY can re-run the London extreme | HTF array unreached on Monday |
| Wednesday Low / High | Mon+Tue hover (can be down/up days) | **Wednesday**; Wed NY can run out Wed London's extreme | HTF array unreached Mon–Tue |
| Consolidation Thursday Reversal | Consolidation Mon–Wed | **Thursday**, on news/rate release, "late New York session… around 2 PM" | Rate-announcement weeks |
| Consolidation Midweek Rally / Decline | Consolidation Mon–Wed, no reversal signs | Runs intra-week extreme, expands **into Friday** | Recently left an array, opposite array unreached |
| Seek & Destroy Friday (bull/bear) | Consolidation Mon–Thu, shallow stop-runs both sides | Friday expansion | NFP/rate-wait weeks; July–Aug — **avoid** |
| Wednesday Weekly Reversal | Consolidation Mon–Tue, drives *into* the HTF array Wednesday | Wednesday sweep then strong reversal | Long-term/intermediate-term extreme; stop-raid pairing |

### Premium/discount execution logic
1. Define the dealing range from the last significant swing high/low that caused the current delivery.
2. EQ = 50%. Bullish bias: setups only below EQ (discount); bearish: only above (premium). EQ itself is "no-man's land."
3. The HTF **array** (FVG/OB/RB/old low etc. inside the discount/premium half) is the specific level the weekly manipulation is expected to reach — the weekly profile is the timetable for that arrival.
4. Weekly PO3 overlay (cross-ref `key-opens-power-of-3.md`): accumulation near the weekly open → manipulation to the wrong side of it (into the array) → distribution. The profiles are named variants of where in the week that manipulation lands.

## APPLICATION TO THE VAULT

1. **Verdict on Mondays: weekly-profile canon does NOT contradict trading Mondays — keep them un-skipped.** The teaching constrains *what the weekly extreme does*, not whether a 10:00 intraday setup works. Dual46 is a capped-1:5 day trade, closed the same session; it never depends on Monday printing the weekly low/high. Even in the classic Tuesday-low week, Monday "hovering above the discount array" is a tradeable drift with a defined draw below — and the user's three daily-bias gates (NWOG → PDH/PDL → Judas/Cont) already re-derive direction each morning, which is exactly the conditioning ICT demands (know the array, then read the day). The June 2W/0L Monday sample is small, but the lore it overturned ("skip Mondays") was never canon to begin with.
2. **One genuine Monday caution survives:** in bullish weeks whose HTF discount array sits *below* Monday's range, canon expects the drive into that array on **Tuesday** — so a Monday *long* that must travel 100 pts against a pending Tuesday-low script is fighting the template, while a Monday *short* toward the array is riding it. Practical rule-form: log "HTF array side vs Monday open" as a journal column; if Monday's bias gates say long while an unreached HTF discount array sits below, that's a flag for the census, not a veto.
3. **Add the weekly dealing-range EQ check to the Sunday/Monday prep (eyes-only).** One question — is price in weekly premium or discount, and which HTF array is unreached? — names the week's likely profile and tells you which side's Judas is "engineered" vs "true." It costs one fib draw and feeds gate #2 (PDH/PDL story) with the week-scale draw. Do not add it as a fourth gate; the freeze stays.
4. **Friday/S&D filter:** on NFP or rate-decision-wait weeks (and ICT's July–August caveat — i.e. *now*), expect shallow both-side stop-runs of the intra-week range. Those are the weeks the 10:00 RB is most likely to be a raid rather than the manipulation extreme — pair with the red-folder stand-down topic (queue #40).
5. **Profiles are measurable with tools we already have.** The twingall HoW/LoW day-of-week indicator (or a 20-line script on the Vault's own MNQ data) can produce the base rates ICT never gave: % of weeks whose low printed Monday, and Monday-session expectancy conditioned on weekly premium/discount. That would upgrade the Monday decision from "lore vs 2-trade sample" to an actual number — a natural Track-B-style side study once the sim queue clears.
