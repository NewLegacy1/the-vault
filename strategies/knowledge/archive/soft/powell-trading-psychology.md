# Powell — Trading Psychology Rules (from the free channel)

Mined from the six psychology-focused transcripts in `vault-app/data/powell-transcripts/`:
"5 trading mistakes that kept me unprofitable", "I asked 40,000 traders their BIGGEST struggle",
"Break The Cycle", "I Quit My Job 2 Years Ago", "Blowing all of my accounts.", "There is a
Different Version of You.", plus beginner material from "Top 5 Mistakes Traders Make".
All quotes are Powell's own words from his free YouTube channel (@Powelltrades).

## Key findings

- **His entire risk-psych architecture is built around one confession: he cannot tolerate large single losses.** The tight-stop / high-RR "bottom-ticking" style exists *because of* a psychological flaw, not despite one: "the whole bottom top ticking thing was created because I could not take big losses. I physically could not take it. I had to lower my stop-loss size" (*5 trading mistakes*). His doctrine: "Tailor your trading style to your psychological flaws… Don't try to fight it" (*5 trading mistakes*; *40,000 traders*).
- **The hard daily circuit breaker is his most concrete rule.** "If I take a win on the day, completely shut my laptop. No questions asked. If I take a loss, I can derisk 50%, go back in the market, take one more high quality trade… If that loses, close the laptop. If that wins, close the laptop" (*5 trading mistakes*). Same rule in *Break The Cycle*: "I can try twice. If it doesn't work out, I am offside. I don't have the right bias."
- **Revenge trading is named as the single unifying enemy.** "Everything can be brushed under the same calendar, it's revenge trading. Anger, greed, impatience, everything comes down to revenge trading" (*Quit My Job*). The trigger state has a name: "If I take a loss, my brain is going to switch into 'I want to get that money back' mode. That is a dangerous mode… please shut your laptop fast" (*Quit My Job*).
- **The turnaround story is always the same mechanism: one month of unconditional rule-following.** "Stick to your rules for one singular month… I didn't even look at my P&L throughout the month… end of the month, eligible for payouts on all of my accounts" (*5 trading mistakes*). In *Quit My Job* the same month is described as "one trade per day, everything with limits… win or lose, closed my laptop." The one-trade-per-day month is what took him from −$13k cumulative to his first five-figure month.
- **Account-blowing pattern he warns about:** beginner's luck → oversizing → blow → immediate re-fund → repeat ("doubled that account… thought I could print money out of thin air. The next day, I blew it all" — *Top 5 Mistakes*; "I blew 15 evaluations in one day" — *40,000 traders*). His prescribed exit from the loop: "Downsize. Use smaller risk. Get more confident in your setups. Take less trades" (*Break The Cycle*).
- **Anti-tilt reframe for "stopped to the tick":** "Every single tick high and low that you see, someone is getting stopped out to the tick… It is not hunting you. Price seeks premium and discount and liquidity and imbalances. Very often your entry is in either one of those. Stop making it so deep" (*Break The Cycle*).
- **Patience/selection style is structural, not willpower:** low frequency, limit orders, off the screen. "I can set limits and I can just not be on the chart… more streamlined than sitting and staring at the chart for two hours until you eventually force something" (*40,000 traders*).
- **Information hygiene:** no Discord/X during the trading window. "Go ghost for the two hours that you're actively trading and don't let anyone else influence your bias" (*40,000 traders*). He specifically recounts entering trades off ICT tweets as a failure mode.

## Details / mechanics

### The rule set (as stated on tape)
| Rule | Quote / source |
| --- | --- |
| Max 2 attempts/day; 2nd attempt at 50% risk | "take a loss, derisk 50%, one more high quality trade" (*5 trading mistakes*) |
| Win = done for the day | "If I take a win on the day, completely shut my laptop" (*5 trading mistakes*) |
| Two losses = wrong bias, stand down | "I can try twice. If it doesn't work out, I am offside. I don't have the right bias" (*Break The Cycle*) |
| One system, minimum 1–3 months before judging | "if you can't stick to it for at least 3 months, you're going to make zero dollars" (*40,000 traders*); "stick to your rules for one singular month" (*5 trading mistakes*) |
| No chart-watching; limits only | "I did everything with limits… one trade per day, win or lose, closed my laptop" (*Quit My Job*) |
| No social feeds during the session | "go ghost for the two hours that you're actively trading" (*40,000 traders*) |
| Don't look at P&L intra-month | "I didn't even look at my P&L throughout the month" (*5 trading mistakes*) |
| Emotions allowed, actions not | "You can have emotions. You just can't act on them" (*Quit My Job*) |

### Tilt prevention — the named failure modes
1. **"Get it back" mode** — post-loss brain switch; the answer is a physical action (close the laptop), not self-talk (*Quit My Job*).
2. **Chasing legs / FOMO** — "every time I chased a trade… I would top tick that long and price would just die"; cured by hard stop after repeated pain (*40,000 traders*).
3. **Break-even meddling** — pulling stops to BE early, then getting ticked out: "it ticks my break even and then it destroys me. That happened more times than I can count" (*40,000 traders*).
4. **Goal-induced pressure** — "when you go in with the mentality of I need this result today, this week, this month, 95% of the time you're not going to get it"; replace outcome goals with process goals (*40,000 traders*).
5. **Deadline pressure / burning the boats** — quitting the job or relying on trading income too early makes each trade feel existential: "you start worrying more about the individual trades because I really need this trade to eat this week — not a good mentality" (*5 trading mistakes*). Keep a buffer or income.
6. **Strategy hopping after 2 losses** — "you're just disguising fear with optimization" (*5 trading mistakes*); "strategy hopping is never going to give you an edge" (*40,000 traders*).

### Account-blowing warnings (prop-specific)
- The blow-buy-blow loop: "if you keep ending up in this loop of blowing and buying and getting funded and then blowing again, please stop. Downsize" (*Break The Cycle*).
- Prop rules break on emotion, not difficulty: "The consistency rule — your greed doesn't like it. The max drawdown — your fear doesn't like it. Minimum trading days — your impatience doesn't like it… You are letting yourself get robbed by your emotions" (*Break The Cycle*).
- Treat a copy-traded stack as one live account sized at total drawdown: "20 accounts × $2,500 = I treat this as a $50,000 live account" (*Blowing all of my accounts*). The frame kills the "it's just an eval" gambling license.
- Don't scale account count before payouts prove the edge: "Don't scale too soon. Start scaling when you have some proof in the pudding" (*Top 5 Mistakes*).
- Speed traps: "You're not going to get there faster because you blow accounts faster" (*Top 5 Mistakes*).

### Habits / routines / self-assessment
- **Addiction self-audit:** "Do you have any addictions — nicotine, caffeine, whatever? …you have an emotional brain… you are going to be prone to gambling. We avoid gambling at all costs. Treat it like the plague" (*40,000 traders*). Everything outside the system's rules *is* gambling by his definition.
- **Learn the lesson before the catastrophe:** motorcycle analogy — don't wait for the crash; "you can learn that lesson before you blow your account" (*5 trading mistakes*).
- **Wins are as dangerous as losses:** "Being in a winning trade is almost more difficult than being in a losing trade for some people" (*Top 5 Mistakes*); post-win the brain re-classifies profit as "your money" and defends it emotionally (*Quit My Job*).
- **Milestones over end goals:** first payout → 4K → 10K month; "slow is smooth and smooth is fast" (*5 trading mistakes*).
- **Nuance-learning only comes after mechanization:** stick to one model for months, *then* learn "when does it work, what news days, where should the stop go — these intricate things only come when you stick to something" (*5 trading mistakes*). Matches the Vault's replay-walk approach exactly.
- Note for honesty: Powell's Monday comment — "trading no-news Mondays, I don't do that anymore because I didn't perform well, so might as well stop" (*40,000 traders*) — is a *personal-sample* cut, the same n-small reasoning the Vault used to skip Mondays and then reversed with data. His own meta-rule ("this does not work → stop doing this") is driven by his journal, not a market law.

## APPLICATION TO THE VAULT

1. **Dual46 already implements Powell's two structural anti-tilt defenses** — limit entries (no chasing, no staring) and a fixed capped target (no winner-meddling). The census confirms the psychology teaching and the frozen mechanics are one design.
2. **Adopt his daily circuit breaker verbatim for live prop walks** — it is fully compatible with the freeze because it constrains *frequency*, not entry/exit logic: win → done; loss → at most one more A-setup at half size; two losses → bias is wrong, stand down. Dual46's one-setup-per-day nature at the 10:00 window means this mostly binds the *discretionary sleeve*, which is exactly where tilt risk lives.
3. **"Get it back" mode is the named risk for the disc sleeve after a script-arm loss.** The rule-form answer: after any Dual46 loss, the discretionary sleeve is locked for the day. Powell's version: shut the laptop.
4. **No mid-month P&L staring during live months.** Powell's breakthrough month worked partly because exams kept him from watching equity. For the Vault: check the canvas/ledger weekly, not per-trade, during live prop months; the journal process already externalizes review.
5. **The Monday lesson cuts in the Vault's favor:** Powell's Monday-skip is explicitly a personal-journal decision, not a teaching about market structure. The Vault's June data (2W/0L on Mondays) is the same class of evidence pointing the other way — his framework ("take the information and apply it") endorses following your own ledger, and says nothing that forbids Mondays.
6. **Prop-stack framing:** when the user scales to multiple copied accounts, adopt the "one live account = sum of drawdowns" mental model on day one — it is the single highest-leverage reframe in these transcripts for preventing eval-gambling.
