# Trade Copiers Across Futures Prop Accounts (Tradovate / Rithmic / NinjaTrader, 2026)

## Key findings

- **Self-copying is legal at almost every major firm; copying *anyone else* is the universal ban.** Apex, Topstep, MFFU, and Tradeify all explicitly permit mirroring your own trades across accounts you own. Every firm prohibits third-party signals, copying another trader, and coordinated group execution. The ownership test — "did the trades originate from you, on accounts under your identity?" — is the compliance line everywhere. (Facts, corroborated across firm help pages and vendor docs.)
- **Firm-by-firm as of mid-2026:** Apex — most permissive: copy across evals + PAs, up to **20 funded PAs counted across household/devices/platforms**, same-direction only (no internal hedging), bracket orders (attached SL/TP) mandatory since 2026-03-01, so the copier must mirror brackets, not just entries. Topstep — copying allowed **only via the native TopstepX Trade Copier** (free, built-in): one Lead + Followers, Followers need max-position ≥ Lead, **Live Funded accounts cannot follow**, manual order edits disabled on Followers while active; reported no-VPS/no-VPN restriction. MFFU — allowed across all account types (primary help-center article), Tradesyncer is the endorsed partner, Tradovate's built-in group feature also fine; firm loudly disclaims all liability and won't reset accounts broken by a copier. **TPT — the outlier: bans bots and automated copiers outright**; only manual replication across ≤5 PRO/PRO+ accounts, impractical at scale. (Facts; TPT/Topstep details from secondary sources — verify on firm help centers before relying.)
- **Cross-firm copying (e.g., leader on Apex, follower on MFFU) is mechanically possible and generally tolerated** when all accounts are yours, but each firm enforces its own rules per account, and some restrict copying during evaluation. Detection systems hunt *collusion*, not copiers: shared IP/device across *different people* and identical sub-second fills across unrelated owners are the flags that end accounts. One person copying their own 15 accounts from one PC is the tolerated pattern. (Fact + vendor-sourced detection detail; the "~10 ms fill matching" figure is illustrative, not a published firm algorithm.)
- **The thing that actually kills copy-stacks is fill divergence, not compliance.** Worked example from vendor education (arithmetic checks out): 10 Apex 50k accounts, identical plan; one follower routinely fills 1–2 ticks worse; after a −$2,300 day the leader survives $200 above its $2,500 trailing floor while the laggard, carrying ~$220 of cumulative worse fills, breaches at −$2,520. Nine live, one dead, zero strategy error. Per-account trailing drawdown means the copier does not homogenize outcomes — it homogenizes *decisions*.
- **Two copier architectures:** (1) local NinjaTrader add-ons (Replikanto ~$149–299 one-time-ish, Copilink) — lowest latency (~2–15 ms), require your machine/VPS running; (2) cloud copiers (Tradesyncer, Tradecopia $50–150/mo, SyncFutures $39–129/mo, PropSyncX, TradeDupe, Thor) — connect via Tradovate OAuth/API or Rithmic, run 24/7, support TradingView webhooks. Flat-rate unlimited-follower plans (~$50–130/mo) dominate; Topstep's native copier and Apex's WealthCharts copier are free. (Facts from vendor pricing pages — marketing-biased, but prices are prices.)

## Details / mechanics

**How they work.** You designate one leader (master) account; the copier watches its orders or fills via the broker API and reproduces them in follower accounts with per-follower multipliers (0.1×–100×), symbol mapping (NQ→MNQ, ES→MES), and per-account caps. Two mirroring styles matter enormously for a limit-entry strategy:
- *Order mirroring*: the follower gets the same resting limit order. Followers keep limit-fill economics but can be **left unfilled** when the leader fills (different queue position — the leader might be filled on a touch while followers sit unexecuted).
- *Fill mirroring*: the copier waits for the leader's fill, then sends **market orders** to followers. Everyone gets in, but followers pay spread + slippage — the limit-entry edge is converted into market execution on N−1 accounts. TradeDupe (Tradovate) advertises fill-mirroring including bracket TP/SL.
Which style a product uses is often buried in docs; it is the single most important product question for this strategy.

**Ecosystem mapping (2026).** Apex, Topstep, Tradeify, MFFU, OneUp are Tradovate-brokered (NinjaTrader connects via the native "NinjaTrader" connection; Rithmic unavailable). Earn2Trade and some others are Rithmic (NinjaTrader via Rithmic Plugin Mode; Tradovate web won't work). Topstep's day-to-day platform is TopstepX (ProjectX). Apex historically offered Rithmic/Tradovate/WealthCharts; its 20-PA cap counts across all platforms combined. Tradovate's API allows many simultaneous account connections; Rithmic's adapter has session limits — copiers scale more easily on Tradovate stacks.

**Compliance rules that interact with copying:**
- Apex: same-side-only across your accounts (no hedging one PA against another); consistency (50% under 4.0 rules) applies **per account independently** — copying identical trades means every PA has the same largest-day ratio, so one big day blocks payout on *all* of them simultaneously; bracket-order mandate means bare market entries get rejected.
- Topstep: Lead must have the smallest max position size; clearing the copier with open positions liquidates them; copier auto-disables during payout processing (reported).
- MFFU: "no two traders on the same device" — a ban on cross-person copying, not on your own parallel accounts.
- Everywhere: exceeding account caps (Apex >20 PAs) can void payout eligibility on the entire cluster.

**Practical risk inventory:** (1) partial-fill/no-fill divergence on limit entries (worst for this strategy); (2) tick-level slippage compounding into one account breaching while others survive; (3) copier/API outage leaving followers naked in a position (cloud copiers mitigate with auto-flatten; MFFU explicitly won't reset accounts for this); (4) per-account consistency math replicating a single oversized day across the whole stack; (5) contract-limit mismatches (a 5-lot leader fill capped to 4 on a smaller eval changes that account's geometry); (6) roll-week symbol mismatch — leader on the new front month, a follower platform still defaulting to the expiring contract.

**Cost summary (2026, vendor-listed):** SyncFutures $39/mo (3 connections) → $129/mo (unlimited/200); Tradecopia $49.99–149.99/mo flat, unlimited followers; Tradesyncer ~$40–150/mo tiers; Replikanto $149–299; Copilink "professional tier" (undisclosed, trial available); TopstepX copier $0; Apex WealthCharts copier $0 with active subscription; Tradovate built-in group-trading feature $0. At 3–5 accounts, ~$50/mo flat-rate cloud is the typical spend — small next to eval/activation fees.

**Trust flags.** Nearly every source below is a copier vendor or affiliate site with an obvious incentive to say "copying is allowed, buy our tool." The MFFU help-center article is the only primary firm source retrieved this cycle. Apex's copy rules have changed repeatedly (30%→50% consistency, bracket mandate, one-direction rule reportedly relaxed under 4.0 — conflicting reports); **pull Apex's own support page the week you set up a copier.** Topstep no-VPS/no-VPN and TPT "manual-only" readings are secondary-sourced.

## APPLICATION TO THE VAULT

- **Rejection-block LIMIT entries are the pathological case for copiers.** The strategy's fills depend on queue position at a specific price. Order-mirroring copiers will produce *fill-count divergence* (leader filled, follower untouched — different trade histories per account, not just different prices). Fill-mirroring copiers get everyone in but convert N−1 accounts to market entries, eating 1–2 ticks (MNQ: $0.50–1.00/contract, but on NQ $5–10) and worsening the measured limit-fill edge. Before stacking, the event-study fill model (see `limit-order-fill-modeling-queue-position.md`) must be re-run with follower economics: either P(fill) < leader's, or entry price = touch + slippage.
- **Per-account trailing drawdown means MC survival curves do NOT multiply cleanly.** N copied accounts are near-perfectly correlated in decisions but not in P&L paths; the weakest-routed account breaches first. Path MC for a stack should simulate per-account fill noise (±1–2 ticks on entry, occasional missed fills) around the common signal, not N identical paths.
- **Firm choice interacts with the copier decision** (extends `prop-firm-landscape-2026.md` ranking): MFFU explicitly allows external copiers (primary source) and TPT effectively forbids them — if the plan is 2–3 stacked accounts, TPT PRO+ works as a *destination single seat*, not a stack member. Topstep stacking locks you into the native TopstepX copier (fine, free, but Lead-smallest-size rule constrains sizing). Apex stacks are the industry default but bring the bracket-order mandate: any copier used must mirror the attached stop, which the Powell-style rejection-block workflow already uses anyway.
- **Consistency replication is a scheduling constraint**: one +5R morning replicated across 5 Apex PAs makes *all five* fail the 50% largest-day test at once — payout timing degrades in lockstep. Firms without funded consistency (MFFU Rapid/Pro, TPT, Topstep Standard) don't have this stack-wide failure mode.
- **Concrete near-term action**: nothing to buy while still in bar-replay walks. When forward-testing live on 1 account, log fill quality (limit touch vs fill) — that data directly parameterizes the follower-divergence MC before any second account is purchased.
